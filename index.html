Your Wizard

Spellstorm Wizard
A wandering caster who channels whatever wand fate hands them. Rarer wands hum with visibly stronger auras — watch the tip of your wand in-run to see it change.
Shown alongside all three spectral companions — Spectral Blade, Spirit Archer, and Bone Hound — though only one joins you on any given run.

The Realm, Explained

The Wizard

You have health in red, mana in blue, and experience in green. Kill enemies to earn XP and gold. When you level up, you pick a permanent upgrade.

Damage and Crits

A critical hit flashes gold on screen. The color of each damage number tells you how the hit landed. Red means it was super effective. Grey means the enemy resisted it. Gold means it was a critical hit.

Elements

Every wand and every enemy has an element. If you match well, you hit harder. If you mismatch, you hit softer.

Fire beats Nature. Frost beats Storm. Storm beats Frost. Nature beats Storm. Arcane ignores the chart and always hits true.

Enemies

Wolves dash at you. Slimes hop around. Sporelings and golems shoot projectiles. Gargoyles fly. Elites glow gold, hit harder, and drop more loot.

Your Wand

Left click is your main attack. Wands are no longer bought with gold — you start with a plain, unbreakable Wood Wand, and every other wand is a random drop off enemies, in six rarities: Common, Uncommon, Rare, Epic, Legendary, and Mythic. Higher rarities hit harder, cool down faster, crit more, and last longer. Every wand but the starting one has limited durability — fire it enough and it shatters, dropping you back to the Wood Wand until you find another. Picking up a wand that isn't an upgrade scraps it for gold instead.

Active Slots, Q, E, and R

There are three active slots. Q and E hold offensive spells — Fire Nova, Frost Lance, and Blade Cyclone — and R holds a spectral companion — Spectral Blade, Spirit Archer, or Bone Hound. Which ones you end up with changes from run to run. Level ups offer to fill an empty slot, right alongside your usual upgrade choices. Once a slot is filled, its choices become upgrades for it instead: more damage, wider reach, faster cooldowns, extra companions, and more.

Blade Cyclone

This summons a ring of spectral blades that spins around you for several seconds. It damages anything inside its radius the whole time, including a crowd pressed right up against you. More blades also means faster strikes, not just more sparkle.

Your Spectral Companion (R)

Once slotted, your companion fights at your side for the rest of the run without needing to be re-cast — it patrols near you and strikes anything that wanders close. Pressing R triggers a Rally: every companion instantly lunges at its nearest target for a burst of bonus damage, on its own cooldown. Upgrades add more companions, more damage, more attack speed, more range, or a unique trick per type — a second strike, a slowing hit, or life drained back to you.

Random Events

Every couple of minutes something breaks the rhythm: a wandering Merchant to trade gold for favors, a fleeing Treasure Goblin worth chasing down, a Meteor Shower raining AoE damage, a Blood Moon that speeds up and enriches everything, a stretch of Double XP, a swarm of glittering Golden Enemies, or a sudden Miniboss Invasion. A banner announces each one as it starts.

Origins

Each run starts with an Origin, which gives you a boon and a curse. Glass Savant hits hard but is fragile. Feral Conduit casts on its own but aims loosely. Pick your poison.

Spell Mutations

Most level ups offer a mutation. Mutations include split shots, chain lightning, homing, ricochet, burn trails, and more, and they stack on any wand. Risk mutations hit harder, but come with a real downside.

Corruption

Corruption is a meter that rises as you gain power. At 33%, 66%, and 100%, the run transforms. You get stronger, and so do your enemies.

The Landscape (Journey mode)

The climb is not a straight corridor. Forest lines both sides of the path and winds it left and right as you go, narrowing into tight squeezes and opening into wider clearings. Rivers still cut across it. Wade through and you are slowed, or cross fast over a wooden bridge. Trees block your footing but not your spells, so you can still fight from behind them.

Biomes and Wardens (Journey mode)

There are six stages. Grass, stone, snow, forest, desert, and finally the Stormscar. Each one has its own twist. Frostreach slows enemies but also slows your casting. Sunscar Waste angers enemies but pays out more gold. A Warden guards the top of each stage, and each one tests something different. Some heal unless you burst them down fast. Some fill the arena with hazards. Some resist your strongest element. Some keep summoning more enemies. Some weaken your damage, so it comes down to skill. The Open Realm starts you in the middle and sends the same Wardens at you in a loop that never ends and only gets harder.

Waves (The Open Realm)

Every fifth wave sends a tough miniboss after you. It spawns far away and walks toward you, so you get a moment of warning before it arrives. Every tenth wave, a full Warden spawns the same way, and the screen turns to a blood moon while it hunts you down. Survive it, and normal waves resume until the next milestone.

Boss & Miniboss Rewards

Downing a miniboss or a Warden triggers a payoff: a chest pops up, the camera punches in for a hero shot, gold explodes outward in every direction, and a guaranteed item reveals itself with a rarity-scaled flourish — the rarer it is, the longer and louder the celebration.

Treasure Caches

Any enemy can drop a treasure cache — Wooden, Golden, or the rainbow-burning Mythic. Walking over one kicks off a slot-machine reveal: a fountain of gold, then 1, 3, or with real luck 5 prize pulls, drawn one at a time from wands, gear, power-ups, healing, raw gold, and huge elemental bursts. Higher tiers roll more pulls and guarantee rarer wands. A five-pull ends in a full-screen JACKPOT.

Power-Ups

Short, loud, temporary buffs that drop off ordinary kills and pour out of caches: 🔥 Frenzy (fire rate), ⚔ Might (damage), 👟 Haste (speed), 🧲 Magnet (loot flies to you), 🛡 Aegis (blocks the next 3 hits), and 💰 Midas Touch (double gold). Grab them mid-fight and go berserk while they last.

Kill Streaks

Chain kills without a 3-second gap and a streak counter heats up on screen. Milestones pay out: Killing Spree showers gold at 10, Rampage grants free Frenzy at 25, Unstoppable drops a power-up at 50, and a 100-kill Godlike streak summons a Mythic Cache at your feet. Hot streaks also sweeten every gold drop, and one-shotting an enemy with a massive blow scores an Overkill bonus.

Local Best Runs

Your best result for each mode is saved on this device. Check the mode select screen to see your personal best before you start a run, and look for the New Best badge when a run beats it.

The End

Journey ends at the Stormscar, where the Hollow King combines every Warden's trick into one final fight. Beat it, and the realm is yours.

The Open Realm has no ending. Waves and Wardens keep climbing in difficulty until you do not make it.

Upgrade Paths

Wand Rarity

Wands are random drops now, not gold purchases — six rarities, each with its own passive bonus and durability. Fire enough shots and a wand shatters; you fall back to the unbreakable starting Wood Wand until the next drop.

Q and E Spells

Three spells compete for two slots. Whichever unlock you choose first claims Q or E for the run. Each spell then grows through its own tree of upgrades.

R Companion

Three spectral companions compete for the one R slot. Whichever you unlock first fights at your side for the rest of the run, and grows through its own tree of upgrades — more of them, more damage, more speed, more range, or a unique trick.

Spell Mutations

Most level ups offer a mutation, a way your bolts behave differently instead of just hitting harder. They stack on any wand. Risk mutations hit harder, but bite back.

Stat Boons

Simple, reliable upgrades that work with any build.
